Overview of Hilton

Hilton is one of the world's leading hospitality companies, known for its extensive portfolio of hotels, resorts, and vacation ownership properties. The company operates globally and is recognized for its innovation, historical significance, and broad range of brands catering to different market segments.

Founding and Growth

  • Founded in 1919 by Conrad Nicholson Hilton, Hilton began with the purchase of the Mobley Hotel in Cisco, Texas.
  • The first hotel to bear the Hilton name was the Dallas Hilton, opened in 1925.
  • Hilton pioneered several industry innovations, including the first central reservations office and the concept of airport hotels.

Corporate Structure and Brands

  • Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. is the parent company, headquartered in Tysons, Virginia, United States.
  • As of December 31, 2023, Hilton's portfolio includes 7,530 properties with 1,182,937 rooms across 118 countries and territories.
  • The company owns, manages, and franchises properties under 22 distinct brands, such as Waldorf Astoria, Conrad, DoubleTree, Embassy Suites, Hilton Garden Inn, Hampton by Hilton, and more.
  • Hilton Grand Vacations is the company's vacation ownership (timeshare) arm.

Hilton Hotels & Resorts

  • Hilton Hotels & Resorts is the flagship full-service brand, with over 600 properties worldwide as of 2023.
  • The brand targets both business and leisure travelers, with locations in city centers, near airports, convention centers, and popular vacation destinations.

Vacation Ownership

Hilton Grand Vacations offers timeshare and vacation ownership options, allowing guests to explore destinations through Hilton's network of resorts 5.
